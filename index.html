Concept 2: Services and kube-proxy. A Service provides a stable virtual IP. kube-proxy writes rules in the Linux kernel (iptables) to NAT traffic to a healthy Pod IP.

Excellent detour. The packet leaves Pod A via veth pair. It hits Node 1's routing table. The CNI encapsulates the packet (puts a Node 1 -> Node 2 IP wrapper over it) and sends it. Node 2 receives it, strips the wrapper, and routes it to Pod B.
